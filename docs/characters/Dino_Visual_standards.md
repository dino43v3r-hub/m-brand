# Purpose

This document defines the official visual standards for Dino, the M Brand Companion.

Its purpose is to ensure that every future illustration, rendering, animation, presentation, publication, and promotional image depicts the same recognizable character.

This document complements `DINO_CHARACTER_GUIDE.md`, which defines Dino's identity and personality.

This document defines his visual appearance.

---

# Design Philosophy

Dino should immediately communicate:

- Curiosity
- Trust
- Thoughtfulness
- Warmth
- Professionalism
- Approachability
- Intellectual humility

Dino should never appear intimidating, childish, or exaggerated.

His design should support thoughtful conversation rather than distract from it.

# Body Proportions

Dino should always maintain:

- Slightly oversized head
- Rounded facial features
- Medium-length neck
- Compact body
- Short legs
- Short arms
- Balanced tail
- Soft rounded feet
- Rounded hands

Body proportions should remain consistent across all artwork.

---

# Face

## Eyes

Dino's eyes should always communicate:

- Curiosity
- Kindness
- Intelligence
- Warmth

They should never appear:

- Angry
- Narrowed
- Intimidating
- Aggressive

---

## Smile

Usually subtle.

Occasionally friendly.

Never exaggerated.

Never cartoonishly large.

---

# Glasses

Black rectangular glasses.

The glasses are a permanent part of Dino's identity.

They symbolize:

- Learning
- Research
- Curiosity
- Thoughtfulness

Glasses should appear in every official illustration unless a specific artistic purpose requires otherwise.

# Workspace Standards

Preferred environments include:

- Modern office
- Research workspace
- Library
- Classroom
- Conference
- Coffee shop
- Church study
- Home office

The environment should feel:

- Clean
- Warm
- Organized
- Minimal
- Professional

---

# Signature Objects

Preferred recurring objects include:

- Laptop
- Coffee mug
- Notebook
- Books
- Plant
- Sticky note

The sticky note reading:

> **"I don't know."**

is considered Dino's signature object.

It symbolizes:

- Intellectual honesty
- Curiosity
- Humility
- Openness to learning

It should remain subtle and should never dominate an illustration.

---

# Lighting Standards

Preferred lighting:

- Natural daylight
- Soft ambient lighting
- Warm interior lighting

Avoid:

- Heavy shadows
- Neon cyberpunk effects
- Dark futuristic themes
- Overly dramatic lighting

---

# Composition Standards

Images should emphasize:

- White space
- Simple composition
- Clean backgrounds
- One primary idea
- Minimal text

Images should support the accompanying message rather than explain the entire message.

They should create curiosity.

---

# Visual Rules

Dino should never appear:

- Angry
- Aggressive
- Sarcastic
- Smug
- Fearful
- Villainous
- Chaotic
- Dirty
- Disorganized
- Overly cartoonish
- Childish

Professional warmth should always take priority over comedy.

---

# Emotional Presence

Every illustration of Dino should make people feel:

- Welcome
- Comfortable
- Curious
- Thoughtful
- Encouraged to ask questions
- Safe admitting uncertainty

Dino should invite conversation rather than command attention.

If an illustration causes Dino to appear intimidating, arrogant, distracting, or self-important, it should be considered inconsistent with the M Brand.

---

# Brand Consistency Rule

Every new illustration should be evaluated against one simple question:

> **Would someone immediately recognize this as Dino?**

If the answer is no, the illustration should be revised.

Consistency should always take priority over novelty.

---

# Future Evolution

Visual improvements are encouraged.

Future revisions should refine Dino rather than redesign him.

Recognizability should always be preserved across versions.

---

# Canonical Authority

This document serves as the official visual source of truth for Dino.

All future illustrations, artwork, animations, and branding should remain consistent with these standards unless this document is formally revised.

# Character Markings

Dino's skin pattern is part of his permanent visual identity.

Future illustrations should preserve:

- The lighter cream-colored belly.
- The subtle lighter blue body patterning.
- Soft gradients rather than sharp stripes.
- Consistent facial markings.
- Consistent shading style.

The skin pattern should evolve only through formal revisions to this document.
