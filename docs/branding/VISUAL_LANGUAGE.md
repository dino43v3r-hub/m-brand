# Direction

The M Brand visual direction is calm, thoughtful, welcoming, timeless, and publication-oriented.

The website should feel like a place for careful reading, reflective exploration, and trustworthy conversation. It should not feel like a conventional marketing landing page, sales funnel, or trend-driven tech product launch.

The visual system should support the brand's deeper work: curiosity, transparency, intellectual honesty, responsible innovation, and lifelong learning.

---

# Dino

Dino is the visual companion of the M Brand, not merely a mascot.

Dino should create a sense of presence, welcome, curiosity, and humility. The character supports the reader's experience and gives the brand warmth without pulling attention away from the writing.

Dino should appear thoughtful, approachable, and professionally gentle. Dino should never feel childish, loud, gimmicky, sarcastic, chaotic, or overly promotional.

---

# Typography

Typography should prioritize readability, restraint, and editorial clarity.

- Use clean, durable type choices that support long-form reading.
- Keep headings confident but not oversized for their context.
- Use generous line height for paragraphs and article text.
- Avoid compressed, novelty, futuristic, or overly decorative type.
- Do not use hero-scale typography inside compact cards, panels, or navigation.

---

# Spacing

Spacing should create calm and confidence.

- Use generous outer spacing around major sections.
- Keep content widths comfortable for reading.
- Allow white space to support hierarchy rather than filling every area.
- Keep repeated elements aligned and predictable.
- Avoid crowded compositions and excessive decorative containers.

---

# Color

Color should feel quiet, clear, and enduring.

- Use the established muted slate blue, soft blue gray, warm white, charcoal, soft gray, and warm wood direction.
- Let warm white and charcoal carry most reading surfaces.
- Use blue and wood tones as supporting accents, not constant decoration.
- Avoid neon, harsh contrast for decorative purposes, heavy gradients, and trend-driven color effects.
- Maintain sufficient contrast for text, links, buttons, and interactive states.

---

# Illustration

Illustration should support thoughtfulness rather than spectacle.

- Use Dino illustrations sparingly and purposefully.
- Preserve Dino's recognizable form, glasses, colors, proportions, and gentle expression.
- Prefer simple compositions with one clear idea.
- Keep environments clean, warm, organized, and publication-appropriate.
- Avoid illustrations that make Dino appear silly, aggressive, smug, frantic, or overly cartoonish.

---

# Cards and Panels

Cards and panels should organize information, not decorate the page.

- Use cards for repeated items, article summaries, and bounded content groups.
- Keep corners modest and consistent.
- Avoid nesting cards inside cards.
- Avoid turning every section into a floating card.
- Keep shadows soft and limited.
- Let text hierarchy and spacing do most of the work.

---

# Visual Tone

The tone should feel like a thoughtful publication with a warm human companion.

The site should communicate:

- Calm confidence
- Intellectual humility
- Clear thinking
- Responsible curiosity
- Welcome without spectacle
- Professional warmth

It should avoid:

- Hype-driven marketing language
- Dark futuristic AI aesthetics
- Overbuilt hero sections
- Loud calls to action
- Decorative clutter
- Visual choices that compete with reading

---

# Accessibility

Accessibility is part of the visual language, not a final polish step.

- Maintain readable contrast across all text and interface elements.
- Provide meaningful alt text for Dino and other content-bearing images.
- Preserve keyboard-visible focus states.
- Avoid layouts where text overlaps images or interactive controls.
- Keep button labels direct and readable.
- Do not rely on color alone to communicate meaning.
- Ensure images support the content rather than replacing essential text.
